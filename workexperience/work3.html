The Problem:

Growth & Business Development analysis indicated that one of our products had high product-market fit in a segment we had not catered to previously. However, accessing that segment required building tools that could be utilized by a third party administrator with partial system access (which cannot be fully detailed here due to NDA).

The Team:

Myself as technical QA & contributor of code fixes, a partnering team of software developers, designers and a product manager, as well as representatives from the third party partner

Product Goals:

Increase overall product value by adding functionality which makes it 1) useable for a new user type and 2) better suited to a new, untapped market segment.

What I Learned:

Countless lessons in contributing from conception to launch in the design, functionality, quality and release of a highly visible, massive and complex product. The importance of closely program managing the many steps of that process and strategically sequencing them. The value of pivoting when it makes sense for the team to do so.
